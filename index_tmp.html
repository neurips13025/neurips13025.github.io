A Versatile Diffusion Transformer with Mixture of Noise Levels for Audiovisual Generation

Anonymous submission to NeurIPS 2024


Abstract

Despite impressive advances in video and multimodal diffusion models, generating sequences across multiple modalities, like video and audio, remains challenging. Adding a time axis introduces more diverse multimodal sequential tasks with dynamic generation needs compared to static data. However, training individual models for each task is expensive. We propose a novel approach using a mixture of noise levels. Instead of a uniform noise level during forward diffusion, we propose applying variable levels across both time and modalities. This allows a single model to learn arbitrary conditional distributions. We train a transformer-based audiovisual latent diffusion model using this approach in a task-agnostic fashion and show that it can be used for a range of generation tasks at inference time. Experiments demonstrate our method's effectiveness in tackling cross-modal and multimodal interpolation tasks within the audiovisual space. Notably, our versatile model surpasses baselines in generating temporally and perceptually consistent samples conditioned on the input.



Method


Our AudioVisual Diffusion Transformer (AVDiT) trained with Mixture of Noise Levels (MoNL), excels at various AV generation tasks in a single model, including audio-to-video, video-to-audio, joint generation and even AV continuation and interpolation with flexible input settings. AVDiT operates in the latent space where video and audio latent, are generated from noise via denoising or used for conditioning. Increased darkness in latents indicates a higher level of noise.
